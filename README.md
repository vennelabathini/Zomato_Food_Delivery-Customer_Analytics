# Zomato_Food_Delivery-Customer_Analytics
Power BI · Food-Tech Analytics

A three-page Power BI dashboard for a Zomato-style food-delivery business, analyzing sales performance, customer acquisition and churn, and city-level demand. Amount/Quantity toggle buttons and city Top-N filters make every page fully interactive, turning platform data into a clear growth-and-retention story.

🧭 Dashboard Walkthrough

Left-rail navigation links the three pages, and every page carries an Amount/Quantity toggle that flips its visuals between revenue and volume views. Each page answers a distinct business question.

📋 Sales Overview (Page 1)

The commercial home page. A branded header sets the scene — the platform spans 150K+ cities and 77K+ users across 150K completed orders — while headline cards report ₹987M in sales, 2M in quantity, 150K orders, and 148K ratings. Three category cards break performance down by cuisine: Veg leads at 122M (12K ratings), Non-Veg follows at 106M (10K), and Other trails at 24M. An "All Sales City Amount" bar with a Top-N selector (Top 5 → Top 100) ranks the strongest cities — Tirupati leads at 43M — and a Yearly Sale Trend traces the platform's rise and fall from 2017 to 2020.

👤 User Analysis (Page 2)

The retention story. Cards track 78K active users, a 100K user base, 148K ratings, and 150K orders, but the two panels that matter most sit side by side: Gained Users (12K — 6.5K male, 5.1K female) against Lost Users (59K — 34K male, 25K female). A Total-Users-by-Age histogram shows the base skewing young, peaking around ages 22–25 then tapering with age — pinpointing exactly who the platform is winning and losing.

🏙️ City Analysis (Page 3)

The geographic deep-dive. A sortable city table lays out sales, orders, and gained-vs-lost users for every market (986M total sales, 150K orders, 11.6K gained, 58.7K lost), while three ranked bars compare cities on Sales (Tirupati tops at 43M), Ratings (Bikaner leads), and Number of Users (Bikaner again). Together they reveal where the platform is strongest commercially versus where engagement runs highest — not always the same city.

💡 Key Insights


Retention is the headline risk — the platform lost ~59K users while gaining only ~12K.
Men are the heaviest-churning segment (34K of lost users vs. 25K women), pointing to a targeted win-back strategy.
Sales peaked in 2018 (₹0.41bn) then declined to ₹0.14bn by 2020, flagging a clear downturn to address.
Vegetarian narrowly outsells non-veg (122M vs. 106M), and the core user base is 20–26 year-olds.
Commercial strength and engagement diverge by city — Tirupati leads on sales while Bikaner tops ratings and user count.

🎯 Project Purpose

The dashboard helps a food-delivery business diagnose its biggest growth lever — customer retention — while tracking sales, category mix, and city-level performance to guide marketing and product strategy.

👥 Stakeholders


Growth & Retention Teams: Target the segments driving churn.
Marketing Teams: Tailor campaigns to the strongest cities and demographics.
Category Managers: Balance Veg/Non-Veg/Other offerings against demand.
Leadership: Track the sales trajectory and intervene on decline.


🛠️ Tools Used

Power BI — three-page report with left-rail navigation, Amount/Quantity toggle buttons, Top-N city filters, KPI cards, bar and column charts, an age histogram, DAX measures, and a custom Zomato-themed UI.

✅ Conclusion

A multi-page food-tech dashboard that goes beyond sales reporting to expose a real retention problem — a strong example of analytics driving a clear business recommendation.
